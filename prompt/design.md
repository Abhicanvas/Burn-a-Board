# Web Design Prompt: "Burn-a-Board" Hackathon Landing Page

Use this prompt to brief a designer, developer, or AI website builder (v0, Lovable, Framer AI, etc.) for a single-page event site for **Burn-a-Board**, IEEE FISAT Student Branch's 24-hour electronics hackathon.

---

## 1. Visual Style Direction (derived from the official poster)

**Overall mood:** Bold, energetic, technical/circuit-board themed, dark mode, poster-like typographic impact.

- **Background:** Deep navy/indigo (`#2A2560`–`#241F4E` range) with a very subtle light-blue grid/graph-paper texture overlaid across the whole page (like PCB schematic paper), plus a faint circuit-trace/blueprint feel in the background.
- **Primary accent color:** Vivid orange (`#F5941F`–`#FF9A1F`), used for the event name, key numbers/stats, and highlighted words.
- **Secondary color:** Clean white for supporting text and headers, on the dark navy base.
- **Typography:** Extra-bold, condensed/chunky display sans-serif for headlines (poster/stencil energy — similar to a heavy grotesque like Anton, Archivo Black, or Bebas-style condensed caps), all caps for hero titles and section labels. Body copy in a clean, highly-legible sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Poppins, or similar) at regular weight for readability.
- **Iconography/motifs:** A small robotic/mechanical arm or claw icon as a graphic accent near the wordmark; solder-point/PCB-node dots and thin connector lines as decorative details around headline text.
- **UI cards:** Rounded rectangle "pill" cards in a slightly lighter navy/purple tone with soft shadow (subtle 3D/neumorphic feel) to display key stats — date, duration, venue, prize pool — arranged in a 2-column grid, matching the poster's info-card layout.
- **Logos/branding bar:** Top corners reserved for the FISAT IEEE Student Branch logo (left) and IEEE logo (right), consistent with the poster.
- **Footer:** Social icons (Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram) and website link (ieeefisat.org) in a simple horizontal row, white on navy.
- **CTA style:** A prominent, rounded, high-contrast "Register Now" button/bar in orange or white-outline, echoing the poster's registration block.

### About / Overview Section
Summarize: Burn-a-Board is a 24-hour electronics hackathon centered on real hardware — not software demos — where teams design, prototype, and demo working circuits addressing genuine rural challenges across agriculture, healthcare access, water/irrigation, off-grid power, education infrastructure, and disaster alert systems. Open to undergraduate engineering students from FISAT and other institutions, positioning the event as a regional technical showcase.

### Objectives Section
Short bullet list (icon + one-liner each):
- Direct student innovation toward practical rural development challenges
- Apply classroom concepts to socially relevant engineering problems
- Encourage low-cost, rugged, field-deployable hardware design
- Showcase and increase use of IDEA LAB's facilities
- Build confidence in prototyping, testing, debugging, and presenting hardware
- Position FISAT as a regional hub for hardware innovation

### Focus Areas for Innovation (card grid, 4 cards)
1. **Agriculture & Water** — soil moisture/irrigation controllers, crop & storage monitoring, water quality/handpump sensing
2. **Power & Energy** — solar charge controllers, off-grid battery/microgrid monitoring
3. **Healthcare & Safety** — portable vital-sign monitors, flood/landslide early-warning circuits, emergency comms
4. **Education & Connectivity** — rugged low-power teaching aids, rural connectivity solutions

### Why This Format Section
Highlight the differentiator: unlike typical software hackathons, Burn-a-Board is hardware-only — teams get full access to IDEA LAB's fabrication/testing infrastructure, mentorship-driven evaluation, and a pathway for strong prototypes toward final-year projects, IEEE paper submissions, or Smart India Hackathon entries.

## 3. Technical/UX Notes
- Single-page scrollable layout with sticky nav (Home / About / Focus Areas / Timeline / Register).
- Mobile-first responsive design — poster's card grid should stack to single column on mobile.
- Use scroll-triggered fade/slide-in animations on section entry to keep the energetic, hackathon feel.
- Keep contrast high (white/orange on navy) for accessibility.
- Countdown timer to event start (08 August 2026) is a nice-to-have hero element.
